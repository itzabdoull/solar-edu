For teachers

Ways to structure lessons and classroom discussion

Lesson sequence

Use the site in order: basics, technology, design, economics, then advanced topics. This creates a natural scaffold from concepts to applications.

Discussion prompts

Ask students why a solar project might succeed technically but fail economically, or why a battery-heavy system may need stricter user discipline.

Assessment ideas

Combine glossary review, end-of-section quiz scores, and a simple site-design or load-audit assignment for stronger evaluation.

For students

How to study the site effectively

Start with the glossary when you meet unfamiliar terms. After each lesson page, complete the interactive quiz before moving on. If you miss questions, return to the section and explain the answer in your own words.

A strong study method is to keep a table with four headings: term, definition, real-life use, and common mistake. This helps convert passive reading into active learning.

Study checklist

  • Review energy units: W, kW, Wh, and kWh.
  • Understand the role of the inverter and battery separately.
  • Practice reading a load audit as a design tool.
  • Use the NGN calculator to connect theory to economics.
  • Retake quizzes until the reasoning feels clear, not memorized.
